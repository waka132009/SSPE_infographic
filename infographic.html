The Spin-Regulated Quasar

A New Mechanism for Super-Eddington Luminosities

The Quasar Conundrum

Astrophysics faces two major questions regarding supermassive black holes: How do quasars sustain luminosities that exceed their theoretical "Eddington Limit" ($L > L_{Edd}$)? And why do observed black hole spins appear to "max out" just below the theoretical maximum ($a_* = 1$), even with continuous accretion?

The Luminosity Problem

L > LEdd

Many observed quasars are brighter than the Eddington limit, which theoretically should blow away accreting gas.

The Spin Ceiling Problem

Standard accretion theory predicts a spin limit of $a_* \approx 0.998$ due to radiation. A new model proposes an additional self-regulating mechanism to enforce a sub-unity ceiling.

A Proposed Solution: Self-Sustained Penrose Excitation

A new model suggests a "Self-Sustained Penrose Excitation" process. Active at high spin rates and high accretion rates, this mechanism operates at the boundary of the ergoregion (where spacetime is dragged) and the inner edge of the accretion disk (ISCO).

How It Works: A Spin-Regulated Feedback Loop

Pext = Peq + PBZ + Pres

Total extracted power is the sum of equatorial, BZ, and residual power.

feq + fBZ + fres = 1

The fractional contributions sum to unity.

This mechanism acts as a "lever" that taps into the black hole's rotational energy to power the disk's luminosity, creating a self-sustaining and self-limiting cycle.

Gas flows in at super-Eddington rates.

At the ISCO-ergoregion boundary, disk energy taps into BH spin energy.

4. Spin Regulation (Feedback)

This energy extraction creates a counter-torque, slowing the black hole and enforcing a "spin ceiling," which in turn regulates the excitation process.

Key Observational Signatures (The "Hooks")

This model is not just theoretical; it makes three specific, testable predictions ("hooks") that should co-occur in high-spin, high-accretion quasars.

Hook 1: High-Energy Shoulder

Predicts a "shoulder" in the 20–120 keV band. This is a quantitative hook: requires a statistical preference of ΔBIC(shoulder vs. cutoff-PL) ≥ 6.

Hook 2: Lag Hardening

Predicts lag hardening: an energy-dependent trend where lags increase with energy (d lag/d log E > 0) across X-ray bands, rather than a simple reprocessing echo.

Hook 3: X-Ray Polarization

➚

Angle ≈ Equator (±15°)

X-ray polarization angle must be consistent with the equatorial plane, within approx. ±15°.

Degree Tends to Rise

The degree of polarization tends to rise with energy.

The Path to Falsification

The model provides a clear path to be proven false. If vetted high-spin, high-accretion quasars (excluding cases with heavy absorption or extreme inclination) fail to show at least two of these three hooks, the scenario would be refuted.

Vetted Sample Criteria

Targets must be vetted high-spin, high-accretion quasars. Exclude cases that are strongly absorbed or viewed at extreme inclination.

Falsification Condition

The model is refuted if 2 or more hooks (out of the 3) fail to appear across the vetted sample.
